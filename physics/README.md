# SAR Study Order

This study path is designed to build the mathematical and physical foundation needed to understand electromagnetic waves.

## Phase 1: Maxwell's Equations

### 1. Gauss's Law for Electricity

#### Equation

$$
\nabla \cdot \mathbf{E} = \frac{\rho}{\epsilon_0}
$$

#### Learn

- Electric charge
- Electric field lines
- Electric flux
- Charge density
- Divergence

#### Why It Matters

Provides the foundation for understanding electric fields generated by radar antennas.

---

### 2. Gauss's Law for Magnetism

#### Equation

$$
\nabla \cdot \mathbf{B} = 0
$$

#### Learn

- Magnetic fields
- Magnetic flux
- Closed magnetic field loops
- Magnetic monopoles (why they do not exist)

#### Why It Matters

Explains the structure of magnetic fields that accompany radar electromagnetic waves.

---

### 3. Faraday's Law

#### Equation

$$
\nabla \times \mathbf{E}
=
-\frac{\partial \mathbf{B}}{\partial t}
$$

#### Learn

- Time-varying magnetic fields
- Induced electric fields
- Curl
- Electromagnetic induction

#### Why It Matters

One of the two equations responsible for electromagnetic wave propagation.

---

### 4. Ampère-Maxwell Law

#### Equation

$$
\nabla \times \mathbf{B}
=
\mu_0\mathbf{J}
+
\mu_0\epsilon_0
\frac{\partial \mathbf{E}}{\partial t}
$$

#### Learn

- Electric current density
- Displacement current
- Time-varying electric fields
- Magnetic field generation

#### Why It Matters

Completes the mechanism that allows electromagnetic waves to self-propagate.

---

## Phase 2: Electromagnetic Waves

### 5. Electromagnetic Wave Equation

#### Equation

$$
\nabla^2 \mathbf{E}
=
\mu_0\epsilon_0
\frac{\partial^2 \mathbf{E}}{\partial t^2}
$$

$$
\nabla^2 \mathbf{B}
=
\mu_0\epsilon_0
\frac{\partial^2 \mathbf{B}}{\partial t^2}
$$

#### Learn

- Wave propagation
- Wave speed
- Speed of light
- Relationship between electric and magnetic fields

#### Why It Matters

The mathematical foundation of all radar signals.

---

### 6. Plane Waves

#### Learn

- Wavefronts
- Propagation direction
- Wavelength
- Frequency
- Phase
- Polarization

#### Why It Matters

Radar transmissions are modeled as electromagnetic waves.

---

## Phase 3: Wave Interactions

### 7. Reflection and Transmission

#### Learn

- Boundary conditions
- Reflection coefficient
- Transmission coefficient
- Fresnel equations

#### Why It Matters

Radar images are formed from reflected electromagnetic energy.

---

### 8. Antennas

#### Learn

- Radiation patterns
- Gain
- Directivity
- Beamwidth
- Aperture

#### Why It Matters

Systems depend heavily on antenna characteristics.

---

# Final Goal

After completing these topics, you should be able to understand:

- Electromagnetic wave propagation
- Radar signal transmission and reception
